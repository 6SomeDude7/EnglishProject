Dear Viewer,

Throughout my American Identity project, I explored the idea that modern American identity is often shaped by hard work, stress, pressure, and the search for balance. Many people in America constantly try to balance work, responsibilities, relationships, and hobbies, all while trying to find time for themselves. I wanted my projects to reflect both the pressure people experience and the ways they escape or cope with that pressure.

Many of my projects use games, entertainment, and technology because I believe these are important parts of modern American life. My Game of Life remake focuses on financial choices, stress, debt, and relationships, while my puzzle game (Connections remake) represents burnout and pressure. My comic focuses on friendship and emotional support, showing how people often rely on others during stressful times. Even my smaller projects connect to creativity and self-expression through design and interaction.

While working on these projects, I began to understand that American identity is not only about success and ambition, but also about the stress and expectations that often come with them. At the same time, hobbies, games, creativity, and entertainment can help people manage stress and express themselves. I wanted my projects to reflect both sides of that experience.

This project also helped me grow personally. I improved my coding, design, and problem-solving skills while learning how creative projects can communicate larger ideas about people and society. Overall, this project helped me better understand how stress, technology, entertainment, and individuality all connect to modern American identity.

Sincerely,
Aiden Hall
